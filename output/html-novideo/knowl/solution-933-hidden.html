  3. Using \(Q\) as the initial point, we move 2 units in the positive \(x\)-direction, \(-1\) unit in the positive \(y\)-direction, and 3 units in the positive \(z\)-direction to arrive at the terminal point \(Q' = (3,0,4)\text{,}\) illustrated in Figure 11.2.6.(b). The magnitude of \(\vec u\) is:
    \begin{equation*} \norm{\vec u} = \sqrt{2^2+(-1)^2+3^2} = \sqrt{14}\text{.} \end{equation*}
