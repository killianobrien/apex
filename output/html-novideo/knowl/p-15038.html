We now collapse the region \(D\) onto the \(xy\)-axis, as shown in Figure 14.6.18.(b). The boundary of \(D\text{,}\) the line from \((0,0,1)\) to \((2,4,0)\text{,}\) is shown in Figure 14.6.18.(b) as a dashed line; it has equation \(y=2x\text{.}\) (We can recognize this in two ways: one, in collapsing the line from \((0,0,1)\) to \((2,4,0)\) onto the \(xy\)-plane, we simply ignore the \(z\)-values, meaning the line now goes from \((0,0)\) to \((2,4)\text{.}\) Secondly, the two surfaces meet where \(z=1-x/2\) is equal to \(z=1-y/4\text{:}\) thus \(1-x/2=1-y/4 \Rightarrow y=2x\text{.}\))
