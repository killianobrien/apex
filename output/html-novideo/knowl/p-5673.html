We try to identify \(u\) and \(dv\) in the integral we are given, and the key is that we usually want to choose \(u\) and \(dv\) so that \(du\) is simpler than \(u\) and \(v\) is hopefully not too much more complicated than \(dv\text{.}\) This will mean that the integral on the right side of the Integration by Parts formula, \(\int v\,du\) will be simpler to integrate than the original integral \(\int u\,dv\text{.}\)
