Let \(z=f(x,y)\text{,}\) \(x=g(t)\) and \(y=h(t)\text{,}\) where \(f\text{,}\) \(g\) and \(h\) are differentiable functions. Then \(z = f(x,y) = f\big(g(t),h(t)\big)\) is a function of \(t\text{,}\) and
\begin{align*} \frac{dz}{dt} = \frac{df}{dt} \amp = f_x(x,y)\frac{dx}{dt}+f_y(x,y)\frac{dy}{dt}\\ \amp = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\frac{dx}{dt}+\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\frac{dy}{dt}\\ \amp = \langle\, f_x,f_y\rangle \cdot \langle x',y'\rangle\text{.} \end{align*}
