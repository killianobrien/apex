The graphs in Figure 2.3.6 shows the graph of the function \(f(x)\) along with the tangent line constructed at \(x=\pi\text{.}\) The graph in Figure 2.3.6 shows the same tangent line and function. Once zoomed in, you can barely distinguish the tangent line from the function. This indicates that the tangent line is a good a approximation of the function so long as we are near the point of tangency.
