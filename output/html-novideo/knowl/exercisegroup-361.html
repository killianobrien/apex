In the following exercises, a planar curve \(C\) is given along with a surface \(f\) that is defined over \(C\text{.}\) Evaluate the line integral \(\ds \int_Cf(s)\, ds\text{.}\)
5.
\(C\) is the line segment joining the points \((-2,-1)\) and \((1,2)\text{;}\) the surface is \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2+2\text{.}\)
6.
\(C\) is the segment of \(y=3x+2\) on \([1,2]\text{;}\) the surface is \(f(x,y)=5x+2y\text{.}\)
7.
\(C\) is the circle with radius 2 centered at the point \((4,2)\text{;}\) the surface is \(f(x,y)=3x-y\text{.}\)
8.
\(C\) is the curve given by \(\vec r(t) = \langle \cos t+t\sin t, \sin t-t\cos t\rangle\) on \([0,2\pi]\text{;}\) the surface is \(f(x,y)=5\text{.}\)
9.
\(C\) is the piecewise curve composed of the line segments that connect \((0,1)\) to \((1,1)\text{,}\) then connect \((1,1)\) to \((1,0)\text{;}\) the surface is \(f(x,y)=x+y^2\text{.}\)
10.
\(C\) is the piecewise curve composed of the line segment joining the points \((0,0)\) and \((1,1)\text{,}\) along with the quarter-circle parametrized by \(\langle \cos t,-\sin t+1\rangle\) on \([0,\pi/2]\)(which starts at the point \((1,1)\) and ends at \((0,0)\text{;}\) the surface is \(f(x,y)=x^2+y^2\text{.}\)
