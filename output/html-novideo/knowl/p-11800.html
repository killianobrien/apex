Since the two lines intersect, we can state \(P_2= P_1 + a\vec d_1+b\vec d_2\) for some scalars \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) (Here we abuse notation slightly and add points to vectors.) Thus \(\overrightarrow{P_1P_2} = a\vec d_1+b\vec d_2\text{.}\) Vector \(\vec c\) is the cross product of \(\vec d_1\) and \(\vec d_2\text{,}\) hence is orthogonal to both, and hence is orthogonal to \(\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}\text{.}\) Thus \(\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}\cdot\vec c = 0\text{,}\) and the distance between lines is \(0\text{.}\)
