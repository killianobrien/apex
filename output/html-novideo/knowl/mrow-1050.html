\begin{align*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\ln\big(f(x)\big) \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty} \ln\left(1+\frac1x\right)^x\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty} x\ln\left(1+\frac1x\right)\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{\ln\left(1+\frac1x\right)}{1/x}\\ \end{align*}

This produces the indeterminate form 0/0, so we apply l'Hospital's Rule.

\begin{align*} \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{\frac{1}{1+1/x}\cdot(-1/x^2)}{(-1/x^2)}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{1}{1+1/x}\\ \amp = 1 \end{align*}
