  1. In an ellipse, knowing \(c^2=a^2-b^2\) and \(e=c/a\) allows us to find \(b\) in terms of \(a\) and \(e\text{.}\) Show \(b=a\sqrt{1-e^2}\text{.}\)

  3. Shift the equations so that the Sun lies at the origin. Plot the three elliptical orbits.
