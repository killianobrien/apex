In Example 15.4.4, we saw that the flux across the two curves was the same when the vector field was \(\vec F_1 = \la y, -x+1\ra\text{.}\) This is not a coincidence. We show why they are equal in Example 15.4.16. In short, the reason is this: the divergence of \(\vec F_1\) is 0, and when \(\divv \vec F = 0\text{,}\) the flux across any two paths with common beginning and ending points will be the same.
