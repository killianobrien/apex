We can similarly plot vector fields in space, as shown in Figure 15.2.4, though it is not often done. The plots get very busy very quickly, as there are lots of arrows drawn in a small amount of space. In Figure 15.2.4 the field \(\vec F = \langle -y,x,z\rangle\) is graphed. If one could view the graph from above, one could see the arrows point in a circle about the \(z\)-axis. One should also note how the arrows far from the origin are larger than those close to the origin.
