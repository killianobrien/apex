We can view the equation \(z=y^2\) as a parabola in the \(yz\)-plane, as illustrated in Figure 11.1.15.(a). As \(x\) does not appear in the equation, the rulings are lines through this parabola parallel to the \(x\)-axis, shown in Figure 11.1.15.(b). These rulings give a general idea as to what the surface looks like, drawn in Figure 11.1.15.(c).
