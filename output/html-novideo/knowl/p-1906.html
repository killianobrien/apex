Let's abstract once more. Let's find the derivative of the general quadratic function, \(f(x) = ax^2+bx+c\text{.}\) Using the definition of the derivative, we have:
\begin{align*} \fp(x) \amp = \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{a(x+h)^2+b(x+h)+c-(ax^2+bx+c)}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h\to 0}\frac{ax^2+2ahx+ah^2+bx+bh+c-ax^2-bx-c)}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{ah^2+2ahx+bh}{h}\\ \amp = \lim_{h\to 0} ah+2ax+b\\ \amp = 2ax+b\text{.} \end{align*}
