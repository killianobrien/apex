Considering Figure 14.7.16, we can make a small “spherical wedge” by varying \(\rho\text{,}\) \(\theta\) and \(\varphi\) each a small amount, \(\Delta\rho\text{,}\) \(\Delta\theta\) and \(\Delta\varphi\text{,}\) respectively. This wedge is approximately a rectangular solid when the change in each coordinate is small, giving a volume of about
\begin{equation*} \Delta V \approx \Delta\rho\ \times\ \rho\Delta\varphi\ \times\ \rho\cos(\varphi)\Delta\theta\text{.} \end{equation*}
