Item 1.
We say \(f\) is differentiable at \((x_0,y_0)\) if, given \(\varepsilon \gt 0\text{,}\) there is a \(\delta \gt 0\) such that if \(\norm{\la dx,dy\ra} \lt \delta\text{,}\) then \(\norm{\la E_x,E_y\ra} \lt \varepsilon\text{.}\) That is, as \(dx\) and \(dy\) go to 0, so do \(E_x\) and \(E_y\text{.}\)
