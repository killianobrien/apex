Exercise Group 27–30.
In the following exercises, a solid is described along with its density function. Find the center of mass of the solid using cylindrical coordinates. (Note: these are the same solids and density functions as found in Exercises 14.7.3.23 through Exercise 14.7.3.26.)
27.
Bounded by the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=4\) and the planes \(z=0\) and \(z=4\) with density function \(\delta(x,y,z) =\sqrt{x^2+y^2}+1\text{.}\)
28.
Bounded by the cylinders \(x^2+y^2=4\) and \(x^2+y^2=9\text{,}\) between the planes \(z=0\) and \(z=10\) with density function \(\delta(x,y,z) =z\text{.}\)
29.
Bounded by \(y\geq 0\text{,}\) the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=1\text{,}\) and between the planes \(z=0\) and \(z=4-y\) with density function \(\delta(x,y,z) =1\text{.}\)
30.
The upper half of the unit ball, bounded between \(z= 0\) and \(z=\sqrt{1-x^2-y^2}\text{,}\) with density function \(\delta(x,y,z) =1\text{.}\)
