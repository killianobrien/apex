Definition 9.6.4. Radius and Interval of Convergence.

  1. The number \(R\) given in TheoremÂ 9.6.3 is the radius of convergence of a given series. When a series converges for only \(x=c\text{,}\) we say the radius of convergence is 0, i.e., \(R=0\text{.}\) When a series converges for all \(x\text{,}\) we say the series has an infinite radius of convergence, i.e., \(R=\infty\text{.}\)
  2. The interval of convergence is the set of all values of \(x\) for which the series converges.
