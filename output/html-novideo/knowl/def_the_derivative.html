Definition 2.1.16. Derivative Function.

Let \(f\) be a differentiable function on an open interval \(I\text{.}\) The function
\begin{equation*} \fp(x) = \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \end{equation*}
is the derivative of \(f\text{.}\)
Let \(y = f(x)\text{.}\) The following notations all represent the derivative of \(f\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \fp(x)\ =\ y'\ =\ \frac{dy}{dx}\ =\ \frac{df}{dx}\ =\ \frac{d}{dx}(f)\ =\ \frac{d}{dx}(y)\text{.} \end{equation*}
