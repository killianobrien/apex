The \(n\)th-Term Test of Theorem 9.2.21 states that in order for a series \(\ds \infser a_n\) to converge, \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty}a_n = 0\text{.}\) That is, the terms of \(\{a_n\}\) must get very small. Not only must the terms approach 0, they must approach 0 “fast enough”: while \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty}1/n=0\text{,}\) the Harmonic Series \(\ds\infser \frac1n\) diverges as the terms of \(\{1/n\}\) do not approach 0 “fast enough.”
