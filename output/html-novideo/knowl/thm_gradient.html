Theorem 13.6.7. The Gradient and Directional Derivatives.

Let \(z=f(x,y)\) be differentiable on a set \(S\) with gradient \(\nabla f\text{,}\) let \(P=(x_0,y_0)\) be a point in \(S\) and let \(\vec u\) be a unit vector.
  1. The maximum value of \(D_{\vec u\,}f(x_0,y_0)\) is \(\norm{\nabla f(x_0,y_0)}\text{;}\) the direction of maximal \(z\) increase is \(\nabla f(x_0,y_0)\text{.}\)
  2. The minimum value of \(D_{\vec u\,}f(x_0,y_0)\) is \(-\norm{\nabla f(x_0,y_0)}\text{;}\) the direction of minimal \(z\) increase is \(-\nabla f(x_0,y_0)\text{.}\)
  3. At \(P\text{,}\) \(\nabla f(x_0,y_0)\) is orthogonal to the level curve passing through \(\big(x_0,y_0,f(x_0,y_0)\big)\text{.}\)
