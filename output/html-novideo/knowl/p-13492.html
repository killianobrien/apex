The theorems assure us that essentially all functions that we see in the course of our studies here are differentiable (and hence continuous) on their natural domains. There is a difference between Definition 13.4.3 and Theorem 13.4.6, though: it is possible for a function \(f\) to be differentiable yet \(f_x\) and/or \(f_y\) is not continuous. Such strange behavior of functions is a source of delight for many mathematicians.
