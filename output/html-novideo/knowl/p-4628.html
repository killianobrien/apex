There are three common ways to determine the height of these rectangles: the Left Hand Rule, the Right Hand Rule, and the Midpoint Rule. The Left Hand Rule says to evaluate the function at the left-hand endpoint of the subinterval and make the rectangle that height. In Figure 5.3.3, the rectangle drawn on the interval \([2,3]\) has height determined by the Left Hand Rule; it has a height of \(f(2)\text{.}\) (The rectangle is labeled “LHR.”)
