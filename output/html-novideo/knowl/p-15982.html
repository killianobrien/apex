Examples 15.5.3 and Example 15.5.5 demonstrate an important principle when parametrizing surfaces given in the form \(z=f(x,y)\) over a region \(R\text{:}\) if one can determine \(x\) and \(y\) in terms of \(u\) and \(v\text{,}\) then \(z\) follows directly as \(z=f(x,y)\text{.}\)
