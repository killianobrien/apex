Now apply the del operator \(\nabla\) to vector fields. Let \(\vec F = \langle x+\sin y,y^2+z,x^2\rangle\text{.}\) We can use vector operations and find the dot product of \(\nabla\) and \(\vec F\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \nabla \cdot \vec F \amp = \left\langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial}{\partial y},\frac{\partial}{\partial z}\right\rangle\cdot \langle x+\sin y,y^2+z,x^2\rangle\\ \amp = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(x+\sin y)+ \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(y^2+z) + \frac{\partial}{\partial z}(x^2)\\ \amp =1+2y\text{.} \end{align*}
