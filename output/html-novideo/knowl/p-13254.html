Another way to interpret partial derivatives is in terms of the tangent plane. Consider the graph of a function \(f(x,y)\text{,}\) such as the one in FigureÂ 13.3.1. Setting \(x=a\text{,}\) \(y=b\) defines a point \((a,b,f(a,b))\) on the graph. Through the point \((a,b)\text{,}\) we have the lines \(x=a+s, y=b\text{,}\) and \(x=a, y=b+t\text{,}\) parallel to the \(x\) and \(y\) axes, respectively (where \(s,t\) are parameters).
