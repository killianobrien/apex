When optimizing functions of one variable such as \(y=f(x)\text{,}\) we made use of Theorem 3.1.3, the Extreme Value Theorem, that said that over a closed interval \(I=[a,b]\text{,}\) a continuous function has both a maximum and minimum value. To find these maximum and minimum values, we evaluated \(f\) at all critical points in the interval, as well as at the endpoints (the “boundary”) of the interval.
