Exercise Group 25–28.
In the following exercises, a convergent alternating series is given along with its sum and a value of \(\varepsilon\text{.}\) Use Theorem 9.5.6 to find \(n\) such that the \(n\)th partial sum of the series is within \(\varepsilon\) of the sum of the series.
25.
\(\ds \infser \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^4} = \frac{7\pi^4}{720}\text{,}\) \(\varepsilon = 0.001\)
26.
\(\ds \infser[0] \frac{(-1)^{n}}{n!} = \frac1e\text{,}\) \(\varepsilon = 0.0001\)
27.
\(\ds \infser[0] \frac{(-1)^{n}}{2n+1}=\frac{\pi}4\text{,}\) \(\varepsilon = 0.001\)
28.
\(\ds \infser[0] \frac{(-1)^{n}}{(2n)!}=\cos(1)\text{,}\) \(\varepsilon = 10^{-8}\)
