When a curve \(C\) is traversed counterclockwise by \(\vrt = \la f(t), g(t)\ra\text{,}\) we rotate \(\vec T\) clockwise 90\(^\circ\) to obtain \(\vec n\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \vec T = \frac{\la \fp(t),g'(t)\ra}{\norm{\vrp(t)}} \Rightarrow \vec n = \frac{\la g'(t),-\fp(t)\ra}{\norm{\vrp(t)}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
