Solution 11.6.2.32.1.
The intersecting lines define a plane with normal vector \(\vec n = \vec c = \vec d_1\times \vec d_2\text{.}\) Since points \(P_1\) and \(P_2\) lie in the plane, \(\vec c\) is orthogonal to \(\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}\text{,}\) hence \(\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}\cdot\vec c = 0\text{,}\) giving a distance of 0. Knowing the principles of planes, especially their normal vectors, makes this simpler.
