In order to find the equation of the tangent line, we need a slope and a point. The point is given to us: \((0,\sin(0)) = (0,0)\text{.}\) To compute the slope, we need the derivative. This is where we will make an approximation. Recall that
\begin{equation*} \fp(0) \approx \frac{\sin(0+h)- \sin(0) }{h} \end{equation*}
for a small value of \(h\text{.}\) We choose (somewhat arbitrarily) to let \(h=0.1\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} \fp(0) \approx \frac{\sin(0.1)-\sin(0) }{0.1} \approx 0.9983\text{.} \end{equation*}
Thus our approximation of the equation of the tangent line is \(y = 0.9983(x-0) +0 = 0.9983x\text{;}\) it is graphed in FigureÂ 2.1.15. The graph seems to imply the approximation is rather good.
