The region has two “corners” at \((-1,-1)\) and \((1,1)\text{.}\)
Along the line \(y=x\text{,}\) \(f(x,y)\) becomes \(f(x) = 3x-2x^2\text{.}\) Along this line, we have a critical point at \((3/4,3/4)\text{.}\)
Along the curve \(y=x^2+x-1\text{,}\) \(f(x,y)\) becomes \(f(x) =x^2+3x-3\text{.}\) There is a critical point along this curve at \((-3/2, -1/4)\text{.}\) Since \(x=-3/2\) lies outside our bounded region, we ignore this critical point.
The function \(f\) itself has no critical points.
Checking the value of \(f\) at \((-1,-1)\text{,}\) \((1,1)\text{,}\) \((3/4,3/4)\text{,}\) we find the absolute maximum is at \((3/4,3/4,9/8)\) and the absolute minimum is at \((-1,-1,-5)\text{.}\)
