Theorem 15.1.12. Properties of Line Integrals Over Scalar Fields.

  1. Let \(C\) be a smooth curve parametrized by the arc-length parameter \(s\text{,}\) let \(f\) and \(g\) be continuous functions of \(s\text{,}\) and let \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) be scalars. Then
    \begin{equation*} \ds \int_C\big(k_1f(s)+k_2g(s)\big)\, ds = k_1\int_Cf(s)\, ds +k_2\int_Cg(s)\, ds\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Let \(C\) be piecewise smooth, composed of smooth components \(C_1\) and \(C_2\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} \int_Cf(s)\, ds = \int_{C_1}f(s)\, ds + \int_{C_2}f(s)\, ds\text{.} \end{equation*}
