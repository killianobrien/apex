Theorem 11.2.15. Properties of Vector Operations.

The following are true for all scalars \(c\) and \(d\text{,}\) and for all vectors \(\vec u\text{,}\) \(\vec v\) and \(\vec w\text{,}\) where \(\vec u\text{,}\) \(\vec v\) and \(\vec w\) are all in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) or where \(\vec u\text{,}\) \(\vec v\) and \(\vec w\) are all in \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{:}\)
  1. \(\vec u+\vec v = \vec v+\vec u\) Commutative Property
  2. \((\vec u+\vec v)+\vec w = \vec u+(\vec v+\vec w)\) Associative Property
  3. \(\vec v+\vec 0 = \vec v\) Additive Identity
  4. \(\displaystyle (cd)\vec v= c(d\vec v)\)
  5. \(c(\vec u+\vec v) = c\vec u+c\vec v\) Distributive Property
  6. \((c+d)\vec v = c\vec v+d\vec v\) Distributive Property
  7. \(\displaystyle 0\vec v = \vec 0\)
  8. \(\displaystyle \norm{c\vec v} = \abs{c}\cdot\norm{\vec v}\)
  9. \(\vnorm u = 0\) if, and only if, \(\vecu = \vec 0\text{.}\)
