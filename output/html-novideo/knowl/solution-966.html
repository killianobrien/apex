Solution 11.3.15.1.

  1. Applying Definition 11.3.14, we have
    \begin{align*} \proj uv \amp = \frac{\dotp uv}{\dotp vv}\vec v\\ \amp = \frac{-5}{10}\la 3,1\ra\\ \amp = \la -\frac32,-\frac12\ra\text{.} \end{align*}
    Vectors \(\vec u\text{,}\) \(\vec v\) and \(\proj uv\) are sketched in Figure 11.3.16. Note how the projection is parallel to \(\vec v\text{;}\) that is, it lies on the same line through the origin as \(\vec v\text{,}\) although it points in the opposite direction. That is because the angle between \(\vec u\) and \(\vec v\) is obtuse (i.e., greater than \(90^\circ\)).
  2. Apply the definition:
    \begin{align*} \proj wx \amp = \frac{\dotp wx}{\dotp xx}\vec x\\ \amp = \frac{6}{3}\la 1,1,1\ra\\ \amp = \la 2,2,2\ra\text{.} \end{align*}
    These vectors are sketched in Figure 11.3.17.(a), and again in Figure 11.3.17.(b) from a different perspective. Because of the nature of graphing these vectors, the sketch in Figure 11.3.17.(a) makes it difficult to recognize that the drawn projection has the geometric properties it should. The graph shown in Figure 11.3.17.(b) illustrates these properties better.
