  1. For \(r\neq 1\text{,}\) the \(n\)th partial sum is:
    \begin{equation*} S_n = 1+r+r^2+\cdots + r^{n-1} = \frac{1-r^{n}}{1-r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    When \(r=1\text{,}\) \(S_n = n\text{.}\)
  2. The series converges if, and only if, \(\abs{r} \lt 1\text{.}\) When \(\abs{r}\lt 1\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation*} \infser[0] r^n = \frac{1}{1-r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
