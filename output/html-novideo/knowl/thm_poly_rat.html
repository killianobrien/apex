Theorem 1.3.3. Limits of Polynomial and Rational Functions.

Let \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) be polynomials and \(c\) a real number. Then:
  1. \(\displaystyle \lim\limits_{x\to c} p(x) = p(c)\)
  2. \(\lim\limits_{x\to c} \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} = \frac{p(c)}{q(c)}\text{,}\) when \(q(c) \neq 0\text{.}\)
