You might already see a simple way to proceed, but let us first consider the following: \(\sqrt{x}=(1+(x-1))^{1/2}\) can be expanded as a binomial series centered at \(x=1\text{.}\) We also know the Taylor series for \(\ln(x)\) at \(x=1\text{,}\) and note that \(\sqrt{1}=1\text{,}\) so when \(x\) is near 1, so is \(\sqrt{x}\text{.}\)
