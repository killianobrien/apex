Item 3.
\(\ds z=y^2-x^2\text{:}\) This defines a hyperbolic paraboloid, very similar to the one shown in the gallery of quadric sections. Consider the traces in the \(y-z\) and \(x-z\) planes:
\(x=0\text{:}\) The trace is \(z=y^2\text{,}\) a parabola opening up in the \(y-z\) plane.
\(y=0\text{:}\) The trace is \(z=-x^2\text{,}\) a parabola opening down in the \(x-z\) plane.
Sketching these two parabolas gives a sketch like that in Figure 11.1.29.(a), and filling in the surface gives a sketch like Figure 11.1.29.(b).
