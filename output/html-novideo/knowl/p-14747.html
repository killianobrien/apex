In a discrete system (i.e., mass is located at individual points, not along a continuum) we find the center of mass by dividing the mass into a moment of the system. In general, a moment is a weighted measure of distance from a particular point or line. In the case described by TheoremÂ 14.4.9, we are finding a weighted measure of distances from the \(y\)-axis, so we refer to this as the moment about the \(y\)-axis, represented by \(M_y\text{.}\) Letting \(M\) be the total mass of the system, we have \(\overline{x} = M_y/M\text{.}\)
