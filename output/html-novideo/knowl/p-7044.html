One can generate a solid of revolution with a hole in the middle by revolving a region about an axis. Consider Figure 7.2.11.(a), where a region is sketched along with a dashed, horizontal axis of rotation. By rotating the region about the axis, a solid is formed as sketched in Figure 7.2.11.(b). The outside of the solid has radius \(R(x)\text{,}\) whereas the inside has radius \(r(x)\text{.}\) Each cross section of this solid will be a washer (a disk with a hole in the center) as sketched in Figure 7.2.12. This leads us to the Washer Method.
