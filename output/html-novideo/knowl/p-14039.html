We can use the direction of the normal line to define a plane. With \(a=f_x(x_0,y_0)\text{,}\) \(b=f_y(x_0,y_0)\) and \(P = \big(x_0,y_0,f(x_0,y_0)\big)\text{,}\) the vector \(\vec n=\la a,b,-1\ra\) is orthogonal to \(f\) at \(P\text{.}\) (See DefinitionÂ 13.3.7.) The plane through \(P\) with normal vector \(\vec n\) is therefore tangent to \(f\) at \(P\text{.}\)
