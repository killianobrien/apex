Continuity is inherently tied to the properties of limits. Because of this, the properties of limits found in Theorems 1.3.1 and Theorem 1.3.3 apply to continuity as well. Further, now knowing the definition of continuity we can re-read Theorem 1.3.5 as giving a list of functions that are continuous on their domains. The following theorem states how continuous functions can be combined to form other continuous functions, followed by a theorem which formally lists functions that we know are continuous on their domains.
