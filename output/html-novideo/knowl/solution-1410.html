Solution 14.4.14.1.

We follow Theorem 14.4.13 and Definition 14.4.12 to find \(M\text{,}\) \(M_x\) and \(M_y\text{:}\)
\(M = 1+2+5 = 8\,\text{kg}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} M_x \amp = \sum_{i=1}^n m_iy_i\\ \amp = 1(0) + 2(1) + 5(1)\\ \amp = 7\text{.} \end{align*}
\begin{align*} M_y \amp = \sum_{i=1}^n m_ix_i\\ \amp = 1(2) + 2(1) + 5(3)\\ \amp = 19\text{.} \end{align*}
Thus the center of mass is \(\ds (\overline{x},\overline{y}) = \left(\frac{M_y}{M},\frac{M_x}M\right) = \left(\frac{{19}}8,\frac78\right) =(2.375,0.875)\text{,}\) illustrated in Figure 14.4.15.
