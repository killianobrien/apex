In FigureÂ 2.7.7 we see \(f(x) = \sin(x)\) and \(f^{-1}(x) = \sin^{-1}(x)\) graphed on their respective domains. The line tangent to \(\sin(x)\) at the point \(\left(\pi/3, \sqrt{3}/2\right)\) has slope \(\cos(\pi)/3 = 1/2\text{.}\) The slope of the corresponding point on \(\sin^{-1}(x)\text{,}\) the point \(\left(\sqrt{3}/2,\pi/3\right)\text{,}\) is
\begin{align*} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\left(\sqrt{3}/2\right)^2}} \amp = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-3/4}}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1/4}}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{1/2}=2\text{,} \end{align*}
verifying yet again that at corresponding points, a function and its inverse have reciprocal slopes.
