Exercise Group 29–31.
The following exercises explore special cases of the distance formulas found in Key Idea 11.5.14.
29.
Let \(Q\) be a point on the line \(\vec\ell(t)\text{.}\) Show why the distance formula correctly gives the distance from the point to the line as \(0\text{.}\)
30.
Let lines \(\vec\ell_1(t)\) and \(\vec\ell_2(t)\) be intersecting lines. Show why the distance formula correctly gives the distance between these lines as \(0\text{.}\)
31.
Let lines \(\vec\ell_1(t)\) and \(\vec\ell_2(t)\) be parallel.
  1. Show why the distance formula for distance between lines cannot be used as stated to find the distance between the lines.
  2. Show why letting \(\vec c=(\overrightarrow{P_1P_2}\times\vec d_2)\times\vec d_2\) allows one to the use the formula.
  3. Show how one can use the formula for the distance between a point and a line to find the distance between parallel lines.
