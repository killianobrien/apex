Theorem 12.2.26. Indefinite and Definite Integrals of Vector-Valued Functions.

Let \(\vec r(t) = \la f(t),g(t)\ra\) be a vector-valued function in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) that is continuous on \([a,b]\text{.}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle \ds \int \vec r(t)\, dt = \la \int f(t)\, dt, \int g(t)\, dt\ra\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \ds \int_a^b \vec r(t)\, dt = \la \int_a^b f(t)\, dt, \int_a^b g(t)\, dt\ra\)
A similar statement holds for vector-valued functions in \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{.}\)
