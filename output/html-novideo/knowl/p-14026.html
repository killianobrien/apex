The direction of the normal line has many uses, one of which is the definition of the tangent plane which we define shortly. Another use is in measuring distances from the surface to a point. Given a point \(Q\) in space, it is a general geometric concept to define the distance from \(Q\) to the surface as being the length of the shortest line segment \(\overline{PQ}\) over all points \(P\) on the surface. This, in turn, implies that \(\overrightarrow{PQ}\) will be orthogonal to the surface at \(P\text{.}\) Therefore we can measure the distance from \(Q\) to the surface \(f\) by finding a point \(P\) on the surface such that \(\overrightarrow{PQ}\) is parallel to the normal line to \(f\) at \(P\text{.}\)
