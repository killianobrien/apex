Hooke's Law states that the force required to compress or stretch a spring \(x\) units from its natural length is proportional to \(x\text{;}\) that is, this force is \(F(x) = kx\) for some constant \(k\text{.}\) For example, if a force of 1 N stretches a given spring 2 cm, then a force of 5 N will stretch the spring 10 cm.
