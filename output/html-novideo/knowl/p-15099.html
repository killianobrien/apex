Let \(w=h(x,y,z)\) be a continuous function over a closed, bounded region \(D\) in space, and let \(\Delta D\) be any partition of \(D\) into \(n\) rectangular solids with volume \(V_i\text{.}\)
  1. The limit \(\lim\limits_{\norm{\Delta D}\to 0}\sum_{i=1}^n h(x_i,y_i,z_i)\Delta V_i\) exists.
  2. If \(D\) is defined as the region bounded by the planes \(x=a\) and \(x=b\text{,}\) the cylinders \(y=g_1(x)\) and \(y=g_2(x)\text{,}\) and the surfaces \(z=f_1(x,y)\) and \(z=f_2(x,y)\text{,}\) where \(a\lt b\text{,}\) \(g_1(x)\leq g_2(x)\) and \(f_1(x,y)\leq f_2(x,y)\) on \(D\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation*} \iiint_D h(x,y,z)\, dV = \int_a^b\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)}\int_{f_1(x,y)}^{f_2(x,y)} h(x,y,z)\, dz\, dy\, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
