Theorem 9.6.7. Derivatives and Indefinite Integrals of Power Series Functions.

Let \(\ds f(x) = \infser[0] a_n(x-c)^n\) be a function defined by a power series, with radius of convergence \(R\text{.}\)
  1. \(f(x)\) is continuous and differentiable on \((c-R,c+R)\text{.}\)
  2. \(\ds \fp(x) = \infser a_n\cdot n\cdot (x-c)^{n-1}\text{,}\) with radius of convergence \(R\text{.}\)
  3. \(\ds \int f(x)\, dx = C+\infser[0] a_n\frac{(x-c)^{n+1}}{n+1}\text{,}\) with radius of convergence \(R\text{.}\)
