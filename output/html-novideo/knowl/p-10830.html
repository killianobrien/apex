We study each shape by considering traces, that is, intersections of each surface with a plane parallel to a coordinate plane. For instance, consider the elliptic paraboloid \(z= x^2/4+y^2\text{,}\) shown in Figure 11.1.25. If we intersect this shape with the plane \(z=d\)  (i.e., replace \(z\) with \(d\)), we have the equation:
\begin{align*} d \amp = \frac{x^2}4+y^2.\\ \end{align*}

Divide both sides by \(d\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 1 \amp = \frac{x^2}{4d} + \frac{y^2}{d}\text{.} \end{align*}
