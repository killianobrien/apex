Solution 13.5.3.19.1.
  1. \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial s} = 2xy(1) + x^2(2) = 2xy+2x^2\text{;}\) \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial t} = 2xy(-1) + x^2(4) = -2xy+4x^2\)
  2. With \(s=1\text{,}\) \(t=0\text{,}\) \(x=1\) and \(y=2\text{.}\) Thus \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial s} = 6\) and \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial t} = 0\)
