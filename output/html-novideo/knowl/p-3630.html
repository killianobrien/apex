Solving equations is one of the most important things we do in mathematics, yet we are surprisingly limited in what we can solve analytically. For instance, equations as simple as \(x^5+x+1=0\) or \(\cos(x) =x\) cannot be solved by algebraic methods in terms of familiar functions. Fortunately, there are methods that can give us approximate solutions to equations like these. These methods can usually give an approximation correct to as many decimal places as we like.
