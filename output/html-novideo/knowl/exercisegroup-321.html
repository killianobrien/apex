Exercise Group 13–18.
In the following exercises, a function \(f(x,y)\) and a point \(P\) are given. Find the directional derivative of \(f\) in the indicated directions. Note: these are the same functions as in Exercises 13.6.3.7–13.6.3.12.
13.
\(f(x,y) = -x^2y+xy^2+xy\text{,}\) \(P= (2,1)\)
  1. In the direction of \(\vec v = \la 3,4\ra\)
  2. In the direction toward the point \(Q = (1,-1)\text{.}\)
14.
Consider \(f(x,y) = \sin(x)\cos(y)\text{,}\) at \(P = \left(\frac{\pi}{4},\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\text{.}\)
  1. Find the directional derivative in the direction of \(\vec v=\la 1,1\ra\text{.}\)
  2. Find the directional derivative in the direction toward the point \(Q = (0,0)\text{.}\)
15.
\(\ds f(x,y) = \frac{1}{x^2+y^2+1}\text{,}\) \(P = (1,1)\text{.}\)
  1. In the direction of \(\vec v = \la 1,-1\ra\text{.}\)
  2. In the direction toward the point \(Q = (-2,-2)\text{.}\)
16.
Consider \(f(x,y) = -4x+3y\text{,}\) at \(P = (5,2)\text{.}\)
  1. Find the directional derivative in the direction of \(\vec v=\la 3,1\ra\text{.}\)
  2. Find the directional derivative in the direction toward the point \(Q = (2,7)\text{.}\)
17.
\(\ds f(x,y) = x^2+2y^2-xy-7x\text{,}\) \(P = (4,1)\)
  1. In the direction of \(\vec v = \la -2,5\ra\)
  2. In the direction toward the point \(Q = (4,0)\text{.}\)
18.
Consider \(f(x,y) = x^2y^3-2x\text{,}\) at \(P = (1,1)\text{.}\)
  1. Find the directional derivative in the direction of \(\vec v=\la 3,3\ra\text{.}\)
  2. Find the directional derivative in the direction toward the point \(Q = (1,2)\text{.}\)
