The integral on the right evaluates to \(\ln\abs{w}+C\text{,}\) which becomes \(\ln(1+x^2)+C\) (we can drop the absolute values as \(1+x^2\) is always positive). Therefore, the answer is
\begin{equation*} \int \arctan x\, dx = x\arctan x - \frac12\ln(1+x^2) + C\text{.} \end{equation*}
