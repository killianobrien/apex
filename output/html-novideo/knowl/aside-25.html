Differentials and linearization.

The relationship between the differential and the linearization given in DefinitionÂ 4.4.2 is as follows:
\begin{equation*} \ell(x) = f(c)+dy\text{,} \end{equation*}
if we take \(dy\) to be evaluated at \(x=c\text{.}\)
It is often useful to think of \(dy\) is the linear change in \(f\text{,}\) while \(\dy\) represents the true change in \(f\text{.}\)
