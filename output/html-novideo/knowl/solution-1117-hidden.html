\(a_\text{T} = \frac{4t}{\sqrt{1+4t^2}}\) and \(a_\text{N} = \sqrt{4-\frac{16t^2}{1+4t^2}}\)
At \(t=0\text{,}\) \(a_\text{T} = 0\) and \(a_\text{N} = 2\text{;}\)
At \(t=1\text{,}\) \(a_\text{T} = 4/\sqrt{5}\) and \(a_\text{N} = 2/\sqrt{5}\text{.}\)
At \(t=0\text{,}\) all acceleration comes in the form of changing the direction of velocity and not the speed; at \(t=1\text{,}\) more acceleration comes in changing the speed than in changing direction.
