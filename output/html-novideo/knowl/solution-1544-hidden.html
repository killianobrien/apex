Since \(\vec F\) is conservative, it is the gradient of some potential function. That is, \(\nabla f = \langle f_x,f_y,f_z\rangle = \vec F = \langle M, N, P\rangle\text{.}\) In particular, \(M = f_x\text{,}\) \(N = f_y\) and \(P=f_z\text{.}\)
Note that \(\curl \vec F = \langle P_y - N_z, M_z-P_x, N_x-M_y \rangle = \langle f_{zy} - f_{yz}, f_{xz} - f_{zx}, f_{yx} - f_{xy}\rangle\text{,}\) which, by TheoremÂ 13.3.11, is \(\langle 0,0,0\rangle\text{.}\)
