  1. The volume \(V\) of \(D\) is
    \begin{equation*} V = \iiint_D\, dV = \lim_{\norm{\Delta D}\to0} \sum_{i=1}^n \Delta V_i = \lim_{\norm{\Delta D}\to0} \sum_{i=1}^n \dx_i\dy_i\ddz_i\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. If \(D\) is defined as the region bounded by the planes \(x=a\) and \(x=b\text{,}\) the cylinders \(y=g_1(x)\) and \(y=g_2(x)\text{,}\) and the surfaces \(z=f_1(x,y)\) and \(z=f_2(x,y)\text{,}\) where \(a\lt b\text{,}\) \(g_1(x)\leq g_2(x)\) and \(f_1(x,y)\leq f_2(x,y)\) on \(D\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation*} \iiint_D \, dV = \int_a^b\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)}\int_{f_1(x,y)}^{f_2(x,y)} \, dz\, dy\, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. \(V\) can be determined using iterated integration with other orders of integration (there are 6 total), as long as \(D\) is defined by the region enclosed by a pair of planes, a pair of cylinders, and a pair of surfaces.
