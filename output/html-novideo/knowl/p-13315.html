A brief review of this section: partial derivatives measure the instantaneous rate of change of a multivariable function with respect to one variable. With \(z=f(x,y)\text{,}\) the partial derivatives \(f_x\) and \(f_y\) measure the instantaneous rate of change of \(z\) when moving parallel to the \(x\)- and \(y\)-axes, respectively. How do we measure the rate of change at a point when we do not move parallel to one of these axes?
