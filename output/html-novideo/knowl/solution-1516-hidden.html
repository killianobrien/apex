In cylindrical coordinates, the density is \(\delta(r,\theta,z) = 1\text{.}\) Thus mass is
\begin{equation*} \int_0^{\pi}\int_0^1\int_0^{4-r\sin(\theta)} r\, dz\, dr\, d\theta = 2\pi-2/3\approx 5.617\text{.} \end{equation*}
We find \(M_{yz} = 0\text{,}\) \(M_{xz} = 8/3-\pi/8\text{,}\) and \(M_{xy} = 65\pi/16-8/3\text{,}\) placing the center of mass at \(\approx (0,0.405,1.80)\text{.}\)
