Exercise Group 21–24.
The following exercises are designed to challenge your understanding and require no computation.
21.
Let \(\surfaceS\) be any closed surface enclosing a domain \(D\text{.}\) Consider \(\vec F_1 = \langle x,0,0\rangle\) and \(\vec F_2=\langle y,y^2,z-2yz\rangle\text{.}\)
These fields are clearly very different. Why is it that the total outward flux of each field across \(\surfaceS\) is the same?
22.
  1. Green's Theorem can be used to find the area of a region enclosed by a curve by evaluating a line integral with the appropriate choice of vector field \(\vec F\text{.}\) What condition on \(\vec F\) makes this possible?
  2. Likewise, Stokes' Theorem can be used to find the surface area of a region enclosed by a curve in space by evaluating a line integral with the appropriate choice of vector field \(\vec F\text{.}\) What condition on \(\vec F\) makes this possible?
23.
The Divergence Theorem establishes equality between a particular double integral and a particular triple integral. What types of circumstances would lead one to choose to evaluate the triple integral over the double integral?
24.
Stokes' Theorem establishes equality between a particular line integral and a particular double integral. What types of circumstances would lead one to choose to evaluate the double integral over the line integral?
