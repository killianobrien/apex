For a numerical approximation, make a table:
\(x\) \((x^2+3.3*x-37.6)/(x^2-5.7*x+4.7)\)
\(4.6\) \(3.5\)

It appears that when \(x\) is close to \(4.7\) (whether slightly above or slightly below), that \({\frac{x^{2}+3.3x-37.6}{x^{2}-5.7x+4.7}}\) is close to \({3.43243}\text{.}\) So
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to 4.7^{+}}\left({\frac{x^{2}+3.3x-37.6}{x^{2}-5.7x+4.7}}\right)=\lim_{x\to 4.7^{-}}\left({\frac{x^{2}+3.3x-37.6}{x^{2}-5.7x+4.7}}\right)={3.43243} \text{.} \end{equation*}
