If all one wanted to do in Example 14.6.20 was find the volume of the region \(D\text{,}\) one would have likely stopped at the first integration setup (with order \(dz\, dy\, dx\)) and computed the volume from there. However, we included the other two methods 1) to show that it could be done, “messy” or not, and 2) because sometimes we “have” to use a less desirable order of integration in order to actually integrate.
