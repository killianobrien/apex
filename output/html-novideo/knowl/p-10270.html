The solution is non-trivial; use identities \(\sin(2t)=2\sin(t) \cos(t)\) and \(\cos(2t)=\cos^2(t) -\sin^2(t)\) to rewrite \(g'(t) = 2\sin(t) (2\cos^2(t) -\sin^2(t) )\text{.}\) On \([0,2\pi]\text{,}\) \(\sin(t) = 0\) when \(t=0,\pi,2\pi\text{,}\) and \(2\cos^2(t) -\sin^2(t) =0\) when \(t=\tan^{-1}(\sqrt{2}),\,\pi \pm \tan^{-1}(\sqrt{2}),\,2\pi-\tan^{-1}(\sqrt{2})\text{.}\)
