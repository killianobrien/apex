Exercise 47.
Break the Harmonic Series into the sum of the odd and even terms:
\begin{equation*} \infser \frac1n = \infser \frac{1}{2n-1}+\infser \frac{1}{2n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The goal is to show that each of the series on the right diverge.
  1. Show why \(\ds \infser \frac{1}{2n-1} \gt \infser \frac{1}{2n}\text{.}\) (Compare each \(n\)th partial sum.)
  2. Show why \(\ds\infser \frac{1}{2n-1}\lt 1+\infser \frac{1}{2n}\)
  3. Explain why (a) and (b) demonstrate that the series of odd terms is convergent, if, and only if, the series of even terms is also convergent. (That is, show both converge or both diverge.)
  4. Explain why knowing the Harmonic Series is divergent determines that the even and odd series are also divergent.
