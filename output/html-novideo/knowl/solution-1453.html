Solution 14.5.5.1.
Intuitively, adding \(h\) to \(f\) only shifts \(f\) up (i.e., parallel to the \(z\)-axis) and does not change its shape. Therefore it will not change the surface area over \(R\text{.}\)
Analytically, \(f_x = g_x\) and \(f_y=g_y\text{;}\) therefore, the surface area of each is computed with identical double integrals.
