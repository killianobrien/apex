Integrating in the order shown is hard/impossible. By changing the order of integration, we have \(\ds \int_{1}^{2}\int_{-1}^{1} \frac{x\tan^2(y) }{1+\ln(y) }\, dx\, dy = 0\text{,}\) since the integrand is an odd function with respect to \(x\text{.}\) Thus the iterated integral evaluates to 0.
