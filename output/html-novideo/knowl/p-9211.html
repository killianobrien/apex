The radius of convergence is a value \(R\) such that a power series, centered at \(x=c\text{,}\) converges for all values of \(x\) in \((c-R,c+R)\text{.}\) The interval of convergence is an interval on which the power series converges; it may differ from \((c-R,c+R)\) only at the endpoints.
