  1. Let \(\vec r(t) = \la \,f(t),g(t)\,\ra\) be a vector-valued function in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) defined on an open interval \(I\) containing \(c\text{,}\) except possibly at \(c\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{t\to c} \vec r(t) = \la \lim_{t\to c}f(t)\, , \,\lim_{t\to c} g(t)\ra\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Let \(\vec r(t) = \la \,f(t),g(t),h(t)\,\ra\) be a vector-valued function in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) defined on an open interval \(I\) containing \(c\text{,}\) except possibly at \(c\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{t\to c} \vec r(t) = \la \lim_{t\to c}f(t)\, , \,\lim_{t\to c} g(t)\,, \,\lim_{t\to c} h(t)\ra \end{equation*}
