We can view the expression \(\big(g_2(x)-g_1(x)\big)\) as
\begin{equation*} \big(g_2(x)-g_1(x)\big) = \int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} 1\, dy =\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} \, dy\text{,} \end{equation*}
meaning we can express the area of \(R\) as an iterated integral:
\begin{equation*} \text{ area of } R = \int_a^b \big(g_2(x)-g_1(x)\big)\, dx = \int_a^b\left(\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} \, dy\right) dx =\int_a^b\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} \, dy\, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
