Consider again Figure 12.4.13, where we have plotted some unit tangent and normal vectors. Note how \(\unitnormal(t)\) always points “inside” the curve, or to the concave side of the curve. This is not a coincidence; this is true in general. Knowing the direction that \(\vec r(t)\) “turns” allows us to quickly find \(\unitnormal(t)\text{.}\)
