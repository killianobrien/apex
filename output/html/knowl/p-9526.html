The \(n\)th derivative of \(f(x)=\sin(x)\) is bounded by \(1\) on intervals containing \(0\) and \(\pi\text{.}\) Thus \(\abs{R_n(\pi)}\leq \frac{1}{(n+1)!}(\pi)^{(n+1)}\text{.}\) When \(n=12\text{,}\) this is less than \(0.0001\text{.}\) Since the Maclaurin polynomial of \(\sin(x)\) only uses odd powers, we can actually just use \(n=11\text{.}\)
