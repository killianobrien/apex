\(a_\text{T} = 0\) and \(a_\text{N} = 2\)
At \(t=0\text{,}\) \(a_\text{T} = 0\) and \(a_\text{N} = 2\text{;}\)
At \(t=\pi/2\text{,}\) \(a_\text{T} = 0\) and \(a_\text{N} = 2\text{.}\)
The object moves at constant speed, so all acceleration comes from changing direction, hence \(a_\text{T}=0\text{.}\) \(\vat\) is always parallel to \(\unitnormal(t)\text{,}\) but twice as long, hence \(a_\text{N}=2\text{.}\)
