Consider a function \(f\) such that \(f\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and differentiable on \((a,b)\text{.}\) Note that \(\frac{a+b}{2}\) is the midpoint of the interval \([a,b]\text{.}\) By the The Mean Value Theorem of Differentiation, there must be a point \(c_1\) in \(\left[a,\frac{a+b}{2}\right]\) such that
\begin{equation*} \fp(c_1) = \frac{f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)-f(a)}{\frac{a+b}{2}-a}=\frac{2}{b-a}\left(f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)-f(a)\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Similarly, there must be a point \(c_2\) in \(\left[\frac{a+b}{2},b\right]\) such that
\begin{equation*} \fp(c_2) = \frac{f(b)-f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)}{b-\frac{a+b}{2}} = \frac{2}{b-a}\left(f(b)-f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
But then we have
\begin{align*} \fp(c_2)-\fp(c_1) \amp = \frac{2}{b-a}\left(f(b)-f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)-f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)+f(a)\right)\\ \amp = \frac{4}{b-a}\left(\frac{f(a)+f(b)}{2}-f\left(\frac{a+b}{2}\right)\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
