Item 1.
The equation \(r=1.5\) describes all points that are 1.5 units from the pole; as the angle is not specified, any \(\theta\) is allowable. All points 1.5 units from the pole describes a circle of radius 1.5. We can consider the rectangular equivalent of this equation; using \(r^2=x^2+y^2\text{,}\) we see that \(1.5^2=x^2+y^2\text{,}\) which we recognize as the equation of a circle centered at \((0,0)\) with radius 1.5.
