Solution 14.7.3.35.1.
In spherical coordinates, the density is \(\delta(\rho,\theta,\varphi) = 1\text{.}\) Thus mass is
\begin{equation*} \int_0^{\pi/2}\int_0^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{1} \rho^2\cos(\varphi)\, d\rho\, d\theta\, d\varphi = 2\pi/3\text{.} \end{equation*}
We find \(M_{yz} = 0\text{,}\) \(M_{xz} = 0\text{,}\) and \(M_{xy} = \pi/4\text{,}\) placing the center of mass at \((0,0,3/8)\text{.}\)
