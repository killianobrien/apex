\(-1.1\) \(1\)

\(-1.001\) \(1\)
\(-0.999\) \(1\)
\(-0.99\) \(1\)

For a graphical approximation:
It appears that when \(x\) is close to \(-1\text{,}\) that \(\big\lfloor\lvert x\rvert\big\rfloor !\) is always equal to \(1\text{.}\) So
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to-1}\big\lfloor\lvert x\rvert\big\rfloor !=1 \end{equation*}
