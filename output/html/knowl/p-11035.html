Analytically, it is easy to see that \(\vec u+\vec v = \vec v+\vec u\text{.}\) FigureÂ 11.2.12 also gives a graphical representation of this, using gray vectors. Note that the vectors \(\vec u\) and \(\vec v\text{,}\) when arranged as in the figure, form a parallelogram. Because of this, the Head to Tail Rule is also known as the Parallelogram Law: the vector \(\vec u+\vec v\) is defined by forming the parallelogram defined by the vectors \(\vec u\) and \(\vec v\text{;}\) the initial point of \(\vec u+\vec v\) is the common initial point of parallelogram, and the terminal point of the sum is the common terminal point of the parallelogram.
