Start with a point \(O\) in the plane called the pole (we will always identify this point with the origin). From the pole, draw a ray, called the initial ray (we will always draw this ray horizontally, identifying it with the positive \(x\)-axis). A point \(P\) in the plane is determined by the distance \(r\) that \(P\) is from \(O\text{,}\) and the angle \(\theta\) formed between the initial ray and the segment \(\overline{OP}\) (measured counter-clockwise).
