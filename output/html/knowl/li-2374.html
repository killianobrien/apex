Item 3.
We found above that the particle traveled 12.88 m over 4 seconds. We can compute average speed by dividing: \(12.88/4 = 3.22\,\text{m/s}\text{.}\)
We should also consider Definition 5.4.34 of Section 5.4, which says that the average value of a function \(f\) on \([a,b]\) is \(\frac{1}{b-a}\int_a^b f(x)\, dx\text{.}\) In our context, the average value of the speed is
\begin{equation*} \text{average speed}\, = \frac{1}{2-(-2)}\int_{-2}^2 \norm{\vvt}\, dt \approx \frac14 12.88 = 3.22\,\text{m/s}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Note how the physical context of a particle traveling gives meaning to a more abstract concept learned earlier.
