Integrating in polar is easiest considering \(R\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} SA \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{1} r\sqrt{1+ c^2+d^2}\, dr\, d\theta\\ \amp = \int_0^{2\pi}\frac12\Big(\sqrt{1+c^2+d^2}\Big)\, dy\\ \amp = \pi\sqrt{1+c^2+d^2}\text{.} \end{align*}
The value of \(h\) does not matter as it only shifts the plane vertically (i.e., parallel to the \(z\)-axis). Different values of \(h\) do not create different ellipses in the plane.
