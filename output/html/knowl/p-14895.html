The vector \(\vec u\) is tangent to the surface in the direction of \(x\text{,}\) therefore, from SectionÂ 13.7, \(\vec u\) is parallel to \(\la 1,0,f_x(x_i,y_i)\ra\text{.}\) The \(x\)-displacement of \(\vec u\) is \(\dx_i\text{,}\) so we know that \(\vec u = \dx_i\la 1,0,f_x(x_i,y_i)\ra\text{.}\) Similar logic shows that \(\vec v = \dy_i\la 0,1,f_y(x_i,y_i)\ra\text{.}\) Thus:
\begin{align*} \text{surface area \(S_i\)} \amp \approx \,\text{area of \(T_i\)}\\ \amp = \norm{\vec u\times \vec v}\\ \amp = \norm{\dx_i\la 1,0,f_x(x_i,y_i)\ra\times\dy_i\la 0,1,f_y(x_i,y_i)\ra}\\ \amp =\sqrt{1+f_x(x_i,y_i)^2+f_y(x_i,y_i)^2}\dx_i\dy_i\text{.} \end{align*}
