For \(y=x^3/(3+x^4)\text{,}\) we begin with the geometric series expansion
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{1-x} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty x^n\text{.} \end{equation*}
Note that we can write
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{3+x^4} = \frac13\cdot\frac{1}{1+x^4/3} = \frac13 \cdot \frac{1}{1-(-x^4/3)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Substituting \(-x^4/3\) into the geometric series expansion, we get
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{3+x^4} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-x^4/3)^n = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1^n)x^{4n}}{3^n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Finally, we can multiply both sides of the above equation by \(x^3\) to obtain
\begin{equation*} \frac{x^3}{3+x^4} = x^3\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1^n)x^{4n}}{3^n} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^nx^{4n+3}}{3^n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
