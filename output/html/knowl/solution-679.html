Solution 9.1.42.1.
A sketch of one proof method:
  1. Assume that Theorem 9.1.32 is true, and let \(\{a_n\}\) be bounded and monotonic. Since \(\{a_n\}\) is bounded, it is bounded both above and below. If it is increasing, it is bounded above and we apply Theorem 9.1.32; if it is decreasing, it is bounded below and we apply the theorem. Either way, \(\{a_n\}\) converges and the statement is true.
  2. Assume the statement is true, and let \(\{a_n\}\) be a monotonically increasing sequence that is bounded above. Since \(\{a_n\}\) is monotonically increasing, \(a_1\leq a_2\leq \ldots\text{;}\) that is, \(a_1\) bounds \(\{a_n\}\) from below. Therefore \(\{a_n\}\) is bounded and monotonic; by the statement, \(\{a_n\}\) converges. A similar statement can be made for when \(\{a_n\}\) is monotonically decreasing and bounded below. Therefore Theorem 9.1.32 is true.
