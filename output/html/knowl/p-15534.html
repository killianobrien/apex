  • Let \(C\) be a curve parametrized by \(\vrt =\langle g(t), h(t)\rangle\text{,}\) \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) where \(g\) and \(h\) are continuously differentiable, and let \(z=f(x,y)\text{,}\) where \(f\) is continuous over \(C\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} \int_Cf(s)\, ds = \int_a^bf\big(g(t),h(t)\big)\norm{\vec r\,'(t)}\, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
  • Let \(C\) be a curve parametrized by \(\vrt =\langle g(t), h(t),k(t)\rangle\text{,}\) \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) where \(g\text{,}\) \(h\) and \(k\) are continuously differentiable, and let \(w=f(x,y,z)\text{,}\) where \(f\) is continuous over \(C\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} \int_Cf(s)\, ds = \int_a^bf\big(g(t),h(t),k(t)\big)\norm{\vec r\,'(t)}\, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
