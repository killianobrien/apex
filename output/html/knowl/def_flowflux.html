Definition 15.4.4. Flow, Flux.

Let \(\vec F=\la M,N\ra\) be a vector field with continuous components defined on a smooth curve \(C\text{,}\) parametrized by \(\vrt =\la f(t),g(t)\ra\text{,}\) let \(\vec T\) be the unit tangent vector of \(\vrt\text{,}\) and let \(\vec n\) be the clockwise 90\(^\circ\)degree rotation of \(\vec T\text{.}\)
  • The flow of \(\vec F\) along \(C\) is
    \begin{equation*} \int_C \vec F\cdot\vec T\, ds=\int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r\text{.} \end{equation*}
  • The flux of \(\vec F\) across \(C\) is
    \begin{equation*} \int_C \vec F\cdot \vec n\, ds = \int_C M\, dy -N\, dx = \int_C\Big(M\,g'(t) - N\,\fp(t)\Big)dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
