The videos in Figure 15.6.12–15.6.13 present some additional examples involving surface integrals of vector fields. Note that computing flux a cross a cube requires us to consider all six faces. This is a case where the Divergence Theorem can greatly simplify matters. For integrals over spheres, there are often simplifications possible, especially for “radial” vector fields (those parallel to the position vector \(\vec{r}(x,y,z)=\la x,y,z\ra\)).
