In the examples in the previous section, we performed operations on the arbitrary constant \(C\text{,}\) but still called the result \(C\text{.}\) The justification is that the result after the operation is still an arbitrary contant. Here, we divide \(C\) by \(x\text{,}\) so the result depends explicitly on the independent variable \(x\text{.}\) Since \(C/x\) is not contant, we can't just call it \(C\text{.}\)
