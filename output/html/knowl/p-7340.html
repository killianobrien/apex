FigureÂ 7.4.3 zooms in on the \(i\)th subinterval where \(y=f(x)\) is approximated by a straight line segment. The dashed lines show that we can view this line segment as the hypotenuse of a right triangle whose sides have length \(\dx_i\) and \(\dy_i\text{.}\) Using the Pythagorean Theorem, the length of this line segment is
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{\dx_i^2 + \Delta y_i^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Summing over all subintervals gives an arc length approximation
\begin{equation*} L \approx \sum_{i=1}^n \sqrt{\dx_i^2 + \Delta y_i^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
