We also know that \(\vec w\) is parallel to to \(\vec v\) ; that is, the direction of \(\vec w\) is the direction of \(\vec v\text{,}\) described by the unit vector \(\vec v/\norm{\vec v}\text{.}\) The vector \(\vec w\) is the vector in the direction \(\vec v/\norm{\vec v}\) with magnitude \(\norm{\vec u}\cos(\theta)\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \vec w \amp = \Big(\norm{\vec u}\cos(\theta) \Big)\frac{1}{\norm{\vec v}}\vec v.\\ \amp = \left(\norm{\vec u}\frac{\dotp uv}{\norm{\vec u}\norm{\vec v}}\right)\frac{1}{\norm{\vec v}} \vec v \text{ (Replacing } \cos(\theta) \text{ using } \knowl{./knowl/thm_dot_product.html}{\text{Theorem 11.3.7}}\text{)}\\ \amp = \frac{\dotp uv}{\norm{\vec v}^2}\vec v.\\ \amp = \frac{\dotp uv}{\dotp vv}\vec v \text{ (Applying }\knowl{./knowl/thm_dot_product_properties.html}{\text{Theorem 11.3.4}}\text{)}\text{.} \end{align*}
