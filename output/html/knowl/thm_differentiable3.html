Theorem 13.4.14. Continuity and Differentiability of Functions of Three Variables.

Let \(w=f(x,y,z)\) be defined on a set \(D\) containing \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\text{.}\)
  1. If \(f\) is differentiable at \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\text{,}\) then \(f\) is continuous at \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f_x\text{,}\) \(f_y\) and \(f_z\) are continuous on \(B\text{,}\) then \(f\) is differentiable on \(B\text{.}\)
