Key Idea 15.3.4. Evaluating a Line Integral Over A Vector Field.

Let \(\vec F\) be a vector field with continuous components defined on a smooth curve \(C\text{,}\) parametrized by \(\vrt\text{,}\) \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) where \(\vec r\) is continuously differentiable. Then
\begin{equation*} \int_C\vec F\cdot\vec T\, ds = \int_C \vec F\cdot d\vec r =\int_a^b \vec F\big(\vec r(t)\big) \cdot \vrp(t)\, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
