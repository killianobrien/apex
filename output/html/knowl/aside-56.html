We will be able to prove Theorem 9.2.13 in Section 9.3. This theorem assumes that \(an+b\gt 0\) for all \(n\text{;}\) if \(an+b\lt 0\text{,}\) \((an+b)^p\) won't be defined when \(p\) is not an integer, and if \(an+b=0\) for some \(n\text{,}\) then of course the series does not converge regardless of \(p\) as not all of the terms of the sequence are defined. These requirements actually force us to have \(a\gt 0\text{,}\) since if \(a\lt 0\text{,}\) we'll have \(an+b\lt 0\) for sufficiently large \(n\text{.}\)
