Let \(f\) be defined on an interval \(I\) containing \(c\text{.}\)
  1. If there is a \(\delta \gt 0\) such that \(f(c) \leq f(x)\) for all \(x\) in \(I\) where \(\abs{x-c}\lt \delta\text{,}\) then \(f(c)\) is a relative minimum of \(f\text{.}\) We also say that \(f\) has a relative minimum at \((c,f(c))\text{.}\)
  2. If there is a \(\delta \gt 0\) such that \(f(c) \geq f(x)\) for all \(x\) in \(I\) where \(\abs{x-c}\lt \delta\text{,}\) then \(f(c)\) is a relative maximum of \(f\text{.}\) We also say that \(f\) has a relative maximum at \((c,f(c))\text{.}\)
