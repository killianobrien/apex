The equation \(\displaystyle \frac{dT}{dt} = k(T-A)\) is also a valid representation of Newton's Law of Cooling. Intuition tells us that \(T\) will increase if \(T\) is less than \(A\) and decrease if \(T\) is greater then \(A\text{.}\) The form we use in the text follows this intuition with a positive \(k\) value. The form above will require that \(k\) take on a negative value. In the end, both forms result in the same function.
