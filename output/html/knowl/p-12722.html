The object moves at constant speed, meaning that \(a_\text{T}\) is always 0. The object “rises” along the \(z\)-axis at a constant rate, so all acceleration comes in the form of changing direction circling the \(z\)-axis. The greater the radius of this circle the greater the acceleration, hence \(a_\text{N}=a\text{.}\)
