Consider FigureÂ 11.3.27, where a force \(\vec F\) is being applied to an object moving in the direction of \(\vec d\text{.}\) (The distance the object travels is the magnitude of \(\vec d\text{.}\)) The work done is the amount of force in the direction of \(\vec d\text{,}\) \(\norm{\proj Fd}\text{,}\) times \(\vnorm d\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \norm{\proj Fd}\cdot\vnorm d \amp = \norm{\frac{\dotp Fd}{\dotp dd}\vec d}\cdot \vnorm d\\ \amp = \abs{\frac{\dotp Fd}{\norm{\vec{d}}^2}}\cdot \vnorm d\cdot\vnorm d\\ \amp = \frac{\abs{\dotp Fd}}{\norm{\vec{d}}^2}\norm{\vec{d}}^2\\ \amp = \abs{\dotp Fd}\text{.} \end{align*}
