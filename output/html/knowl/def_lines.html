Definition 11.5.4. Equations of Lines in Space.

Consider the line in space that passes through \(\vec p = \la x_0,y_0,z_0\ra\) in the direction of \(\vec d = \la a,b,c\ra\text{.}\)
  1. The vector equation of the line is
    \begin{equation*} \vec \ell(t) = \vec p+t\vec d\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. The parametric equations of the line are
    \begin{equation*} x = x_0+at, y=y_0+bt, z = z_0+ct\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. The symmetric equations of the line are
    \begin{equation*} \frac{x-x_0}{a} = \frac{y-y_0}{b}=\frac{z-z_0}{c}\text{.} \end{equation*}
