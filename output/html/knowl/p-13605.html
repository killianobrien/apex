Distance of the projectile is a function of two variables (leaving \(t=3\)): \(D(v_0,\theta) = 3v_0\cos(\theta)\text{.}\) The total differential of \(D\) is \(dD = 3\cos(\theta) dv_0-3v_0\sin(\theta) d\theta\text{.}\) The coefficient of \(d\theta\) has a much greater magnitude than the coefficient of \(dv_0\text{,}\) so a small change in the angle of elevation has a much greater effect on distance traveled than a small change in initial velocity.
