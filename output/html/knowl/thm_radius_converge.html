Theorem 9.6.4. Convergence of Power Series.

Let a power series \(\ds \infser[0] a_n(x-c)^n\) be given. Then one of the following is true:
  1. The series converges only at \(x=c\text{.}\)
  2. There is an \(R \gt 0\) such that the series converges for all \(x\) in \((c-R,c+R)\) and diverges for all \(x\lt c-R\) and \(x \gt c+R\text{.}\)
  3. The series converges for all \(x\text{.}\)
