In the following exercises, a surface \(\surfaceS\) and a vector field \(\vec F\) are given. Compute the flux of \(\vec F\) across \(\surfaceS\text{.}\) (If \(\surfaceS\) is not a closed surface, choose \(\vec n\) so that it has a positive \(z\)-component, unless otherwise indicated.)
7.
\(\surfaceS\) is the plane \(f(x,y) = 3x+y\) on \(0\leq x\leq 1\text{,}\) \(1\leq y\leq 4\text{;}\) \(\vec F = \langle x^2,-z,2y\rangle\text{.}\)
8.
\(\surfaceS\) is the plane \(f(x,y) = 8-x-y\) over the triangle with vertices at \((0,0)\text{,}\) \((1,0)\) and \((1,5)\text{;}\) \(\vec F = \langle 3,1,2\rangle\text{.}\)
9.
\(\surfaceS\) is the paraboloid \(f(x,y) = x^2+y^2\) over the unit disk; \(\vec F = \langle 1,0,0\rangle\text{.}\)
10.
\(\surfaceS\) is the unit sphere; \(\vec F = \langle y-z,z-x,x-y\rangle\text{.}\)
11.
\(\surfaceS\) is the square in space with corners at \((0,0,0)\text{,}\) \((1,0,0)\text{,}\) \((1,0,1)\) and \((0,0,1)\) (choose \(\vec n\) such that it has a positive \(y\)-component); \(\vec F = \langle 0,-z,y\rangle\text{.}\)
12.
\(\surfaceS\) is the disk in the \(yz\)-plane with radius 1, centered at \((0,1,1)\) (choose \(\vec n\) such that it has a positive \(x\)-component); \(\vec F = \langle y,z,x\rangle\text{.}\)
13.
\(\surfaceS\) is the closed surface composed of \(\surfaceS_1\text{,}\) whose boundary is the ellipse in the \(xy\)-plane described by \(\frac{x^2}{25}+\frac{y^2}9 = 1\) and \(\surfaceS_2\text{,}\) part of the elliptical paraboloid \(f(x,y) = 1-\frac{x^2}{25}-\frac{y^2}9\) (see graph); \(\vec F = \langle 5,2,3\rangle\text{.}\)
