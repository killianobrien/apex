For \(\surfaceS_1\text{,}\) we need to use \(\vec r_{1v}\times\vec r_{1u} = \langle 2(u^2-1),0,1-u^2\rangle\text{.}\) (Note the \(z\)-component is nonnegative as \(u\leq 1\text{,}\) therefore this vector always points up, meaning to the outside, of \(\surfaceS\text{.}\)) The flux across \(\surfaceS_1\) is:
\begin{align*} \text{ Flux across }\surfaceS_1:\, \amp = \iint_{\surfaceS_1} \vec F\cdot \vec n_1\, dS\\ \amp = \int_0^1\int_{-1}^1 \vec F\big(\vec r_1(u,v)\big)\cdot \big(\vec r_{1v}\times\vec r_{1u}\big)\, du\, dv\\ \amp = \int_0^1\int_{-1}^1 \la v(1-u^2)+u, u^2,4v(1-u^2)\ra \cdot \la 2(u^2-1),0,1-u^2\ra\, du\, dv\\ \amp = \int_0^1\int_{-1}^1 \big(2u^4v+2u^3-4u^2v-2u+2v\big)\, du\, dv\\ \amp = \frac{16}{15}\text{.} \end{align*}
