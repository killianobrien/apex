This notation does not directly inform us how to evaluate the double integrals to find an area or a volume. With additional work, we recognize that a small amount of area \(dA\) can be measured as the area of a small rectangle, with one side length a small change in \(x\) and the other side length a small change in \(y\text{.}\) That is, \(dA = dx\,dy\) or \(dA = dy\,dx\text{.}\) We could also compute a small amount of area by thinking in terms of polar coordinates, where \(dA = r\,dr\,d\theta\text{.}\) These understandings lead us to the iterated integrals we used in ChapterÂ 14.
