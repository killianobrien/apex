Vertical asymptotes occur where the function grows without bound; this can occur at values of \(c\) where the denominator is \(0\text{.}\) When \(x\) is near \(c\text{,}\) the denominator is small, which in turn can make the function take on large values. In the case of the given function, the denominator is \(0\) at \(x=\pm 2\text{.}\) Substituting in values of \(x\) close to \(2\) and \(-2\) seems to indicate that the function tends toward \(\infty\) or \(-\infty\) at those points.
