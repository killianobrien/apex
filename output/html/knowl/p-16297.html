One of the interesting results of Stokes' Theorem is that if two surfaces \(\surfaceS_1\) and \(\surfaceS_2\) share the same boundary, then \(\iint_{\surfaceS_1} (\curl \vec F)\cdot \vec n\, dS = \iint_{\surfaceS_2} (\curl \vec F)\cdot \vec n\, dS\text{.}\) That is, the value of these two surface integrals is somehow independent of the interior of the surface.
