Along \(y=x-\pi/2\text{,}\) the limit is:
\begin{align*} \lim_{(x,y)\to(\pi,\pi/2)} \frac{\sin(x) }{\cos(y) } \amp = \lim_{x\to \pi} \frac{\sin(x) }{\cos(x-\pi/2)}\\ \end{align*}

Apply L'Hospital's Rule:

\begin{align*} \amp = \lim_{x\to \pi} \frac{\cos(x) }{\sin(x-\pi/2)}\\ \amp = 1\text{.} \end{align*}
