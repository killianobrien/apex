In Definition 15.3.2, note how the dot product \(\vec F \cdot \vec T\) is just a scalar. Therefore, this new line integral is really just a special kind of line integral found in Section 15.1; letting \(f(s) = \vec F(s)\cdot \vec T(s)\text{,}\) the right-hand side simply becomes \(\int_C f(s)\, ds\text{,}\) and we can use the techniques of that section to evaluate the integral. We combine those techniques, along with parts of Equation (15.3.1), to clearly state how to evaluate a line integral over a vector field in the following Key Idea.
